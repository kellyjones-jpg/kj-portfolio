About Me

I am a Frontend Web Developer at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) in Golden, Colorado, where I support renewable energy research as the primary coder for various NREL.gov subsites, intranet sites, and U.S. Department of Energy websites. Previously, I was a Web Editor for Leidos, supporting the National Science Foundation's U.S. Antarctic Program (USAP), including a notable deployment to the South Pole to launch a new intranet for station personnel.

My technical work spans web development with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap, Node.js, and XML, creative coding using Processing and p5.js, and data visualization through D3.js, Plotly.js, HighCharts.js, and Mapbox. I have experience with multiple Content Management Systems including WordPress, Sitefinity, OmniUpdate, and a custom ColdFusion-based system. I utilize Visual Studio Code, Git, Adobe Creative Cloud, and email marketing platforms: ContactMonkey and Mailchimp. I also serve as a technical resource for email and list management tool selection.

My interdisciplinary education—M.A. in Art and Technology, Full Stack Development certificate, B.S. in English – Professional Writing with an HCI/UX minor, and A.S. in Business Administration – Paralegal Studies—taught me that creative solutions emerge at the intersections between fields. I approach frontend development by blending creativity, technical communication, user experience, and research methodology to create accessible and engaging digital experiences.
