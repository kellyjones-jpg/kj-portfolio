BioCube

I built a Unity VR experience that immerses users in a one-cubic-foot BioCube, showcasing the effects of pollution in the Tennessee River.

This conceptual VR project allows users to explore a microhabitat designed to examine biodiversity within a specific environment. I selected the Tennessee River, one of the most ecologically diverse yet heavily polluted rivers in the world.

The experience places users in an underwater section near Decatur, Alabama, within the Decatur Flats area. Instead of traditional flora, fauna, and microorganisms, the BioCube highlights industrial chemicals contaminating the river, including polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs), perfluorooctanoic acid (PFOA), and perfluorinated sulfonates (PFOS)—persistent "forever chemicals" that harm both ecosystems and human health.

To contextualize the setting, summarized information about the Tennessee River and its pollution appears at the top of the BioCube as an informative overlay. Each featured chemical also includes a tooltip with additional details about its origins, impact, and environmental significance.

These PFAS chemicals offer no benefits to biodiversity, and public education is essential to raise awareness of their severe health risks—including infertility, developmental issues, and increased cancer risk. This project aims to visualize the invisible and highlight the decades-long pollution crisis affecting the Tennessee River, particularly the Decatur Flats region.
